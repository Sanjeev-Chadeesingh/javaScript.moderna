This is the third iteration of what I now call my war with Javascript. HTML+CSS, the first two arms of web development, allow for a visualized progression through the material: as you learn to style and postion elements, you watch them dress and dance across the browser. I don't know if it would be called practicality or some other euphemism, but the lack of said practicality, the impractical nature of front-end Javascript, is that understanding its initial core tenets is a predominantly abstract undertaking. Gone are the sister browser windows, updated via refreshing and live server extensions. Instead, you stare at the console, either in your code editor or (hopefully) Chrome's browser console. Once passed the core, DOM Manipulation tends to be the first chance to use visuals to express your understanding of the topic, yet by then, I was burned out. Twice. This website will be a working journal of my progression through Javascript, and its styling will be my review of HTML+CSS principles. Please enjoy!
